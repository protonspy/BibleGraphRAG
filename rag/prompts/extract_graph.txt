
-Goal-
You are extracting a knowledge graph from a biblical text (public-domain English translation).
Given a passage and a list of entity types, identify all entities of those types and all
relationships among them. Be precise about the entity types below — the hard cases for this
corpus are Deity vs divine_being, person vs group vs title, and event vs time_period.

-Entity type guidance-
- deity: the God of Israel ONLY (God, the LORD, YHWH, the LORD God, the Almighty, the Most High,
  the Holy One of Israel). Treat all these names and titles as ALIASES of one single entity, never
  as separate entities. Angels, Satan, and foreign/false gods are NOT deity.
- divine_being: a non-supreme spiritual being distinct from the God of Israel and from humans — an
  angel, the angel of the LORD, a cherub, Satan / the devil, a demon, or a false god (Baal, Dagon).
- person: a single named human (patriarch, prophet, priest, king, apostle). Not a deity, not a
  collective people (use group), not a creature.
- group: a collective of people — nation, tribe, family line, organized body (Israelites,
  Philistines, Levites, the twelve tribes, the Sanhedrin).
- title: an office or role ITSELF (king, prophet, priest, judge, apostle), not the person holding
  it. "David" is a person who holds the title "king".
- place: a geographic location — city, land, region, river, mountain, sea, garden as a site.
- creature: a non-human living being with a narrative role — the serpent of Genesis 3, Balaam's
  donkey, the great fish of Jonah, Daniel's lions. The serpent of Genesis 3 is a creature.
- plant: a specific or significant tree/plant — the Tree of Life, the Tree of Knowledge of Good and
  Evil, the burning bush, Jonah's gourd. A garden as a location is a place, not a plant.
- artifact: a notable physical object — ark, altar, idol, scroll, tablets, vessel, weapon.
- event: a named, datable happening — the creation, the flood, a covenant, a battle, a journey.
- time_period: a recurring day or appointed time — the Sabbath, Passover, the Day of Atonement,
  "the day of the LORD". A one-time occurrence happening on it is an event.

-Do NOT create entities for- (model these as relationships or descriptions instead):
- kinship such as "son of" / "father of" / "begat" — express as a relationship between two persons.
- numbers and quantities ("forty days", "twelve tribes") — put them in a description.
- divine epithets ("the Almighty", "Emmanuel") — they are aliases of the single deity entity.
- individual blessings, curses, prophecies, or laws — express them as relationships with the gist
  in the relationship_description.

-Steps-
1. Identify all entities. For each identified entity, extract:
- entity_name: Name of the entity, capitalized
- entity_type: One of the following types: [{entity_types}]
- entity_description: Comprehensive description of the entity's attributes and activities
Format each entity as ("entity"<|><entity_name><|><entity_type><|><entity_description>)

2. From the entities in step 1, identify all pairs of (source_entity, target_entity) that are
*clearly related*. For each pair, extract:
- source_entity: name of the source entity, as identified in step 1
- target_entity: name of the target entity, as identified in step 1
- relationship_description: explanation of why the source and target entity are related
- relationship_strength: a numeric score indicating strength of the relationship
Format each relationship as ("relationship"<|><source_entity><|><target_entity><|><relationship_description><|><relationship_strength>)

3. Return output in English as a single list of all the entities and relationships identified in
steps 1 and 2. Use **##** as the list delimiter.

4. When finished, output <|COMPLETE|>
